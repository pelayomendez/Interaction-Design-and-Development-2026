YOUR DATABASE

Neither is better — they solve different problems. Choose based on your data shape and access patterns.

SQL: structured data with clear relationships (users, orders, products)
NoSQL: flexible schemas, user-generated content, nested data
SQL: financial transactions, analytics, reporting
NoSQL: real-time feeds, catalogs, IoT sensor data
For this course: PostgreSQL via Supabase

Rule of thumb: if you can draw it as a spreadsheet, use SQL.
